    FAQs about Nonwoven

    What is nonwoven fabric?

    Nonwoven fabric is a flat and porous sheet or roll made from single fibers, melted plastic, or plastic films. As the name suggests, it is not produced through traditional textile processes.

    What is nonwoven manufacturing?

    Nonwoven fabric, also known as nonwoven textile, is made by bonding short fibers or filament fibers together through chemical, mechanical, thermal, or solvent processes to create sheets or rolls of material.

    What are the industrial applications of nonwoven fabric?

    Nonwoven fabric has a wide range of applications in various industries, including automotive, apparel, home decor, construction, kitchen and bathroom, healthcare, environmental protection, and filtration industries, among others. As technology and product innovation continue to advance, the applications of nonwoven products are constantly expanding.

    What are the different types of nonwoven materials?

    Based on the production process, common types of nonwoven products include needle-punched nonwovens, thermal-bonded nonwovens, chemical-bonded nonwovens, spunlace nonwovens, spunbond nonwovens, and meltblown nonwovens, etc.
