    SAIL NONWOVEN MACHINERY is reliable nonwoven machinery manufacturer that integrates processing, manufacturing, and R&D. We provide customers with reliable and complete solutions for nonwoven fabric production, as well as technical support to contribute to the success and development of each customer.
    The application scope of nonwoven fabric products is wide and rapidly evolving. Our company is committed to tailoring nonwoven production lines that meet the specific needs and ensure reliable quality for every customer.

    Needle Punching Line
    • Nonwoven Carpet Production Line

      Typically, polyester staple fiber is used as the main raw material for producing nonwoven carpets. The process involves fiber opening, blending, carding, web forming, and needle punching to form the base fabric. Then, various treatments such as velour, jacquard, or striped patterns can be applied, followed by bonding to create nonwoven carpets with different styles or surface patterns.

      Learn More
    • Geotextile Production Line

      Geotextile production line primarily utilizes needle-punching nonwoven technology to produce geotextile products of different materials, widths, and GSMs.
      Typically, polyester or polypropylene staple fibers are used as the main raw materials, which undergo processes such as fiber opening, blending, carding, web forming, needle punching reinforcement, and thermal bonding to produce geotextile rolls.

      Learn More
    • Filter Felt Production Line

      Filter felt production line processes various fiber raw materials through opening, blending, carding, web forming, needle punching, and thermal calendaring processes to produce rolls of fabric. Subsequently, further processing or composite treatments are applied to obtain a more diverse range of filtering material products.

      Learn More
    • Automotive Interior Felt Production Line

      Automotive interior felt production line processes various fiber raw materials through opening, blending, carding, web forming, needle punching, and thermal calendaring processes to produce rolls of fabric. Subsequently, further processing or composite treatments are applied to obtain a more diverse range of functional materials.

      Learn More
    • Synthetic Leather Substrate Production Line

      Synthetic leather substrate production line processes various fiber raw materials through opening, blending, carding, web forming, needle punching, and thermal calendaring processes to produce rolls of fabric. Subsequently, the nonwoven rolls undergo further processing and some modification treatments.

      Learn More
    • Shoe Material Production Line

      Shoe material production line processes various fiber raw materials through opening, blending, carding, web forming, needle punching, and thermal calendaring processes to produce rolls of fabric. Subsequently, the nonwoven rolls can undergo further processing or additional chemical bonding treatments.

      Learn More
    • Nonwoven Felt Production Line

      Nonwoven felt production line processes various fiber raw materials through opening, blending, carding, web forming, needle punching, and thermal calendaring processes to produce rolls of fabric. The selection of raw materials can be flexibly adjusted according to different market demands and applications, allowing for the production of products with different textures, materials, and styles.

      Learn More
    • Cleaning Cloth Production Line

      Cleaning cloth production line processes polyester fibers and absorbent fiber raw materials through opening, blending, carding, web forming, needle punching, and thermal calendaring processes to produce rolls of fabric. Depending on different market demands, various fiber ratios can be selected, and the process can be adjusted to produce cleaning cloth products with different textures, absorbencies, and durability.

      Learn More
    • Nonwoven Acoustic Panel Production Line

      Acoustic fiber board production line processes polyester fibers and low-melting fibers through opening, blending, carding, web forming, needle punching, and thermal bonding to produce nonwoven sheets. Products of different GSMs, thicknesses, and densities can be produced by selecting different fiber ratios and adjusting the process according to different market demands.

      Learn More
    Thermobonding Line
    • Thermal Bonded Wadding Production Line

      Thermal bonded wadding production line processes polyester fibers and low melt fibers through opening, blending, carding, web forming, web bonding, and drying processes to produce lofty rolls. Different fiber combinations and process adjustments can be chosen according to various market demands to produce products with different textures, thicknesses, and loftiness.

      Learn More
    • Thermal Bonded Padding Production Line

      Thermal bonded padding production line primarily utilizes hot air consolidation, also known as thermal bonding technology, to manufacture nonwoven materials with a high-density three-dimensional network structure, commonly referred to as padding.

      Learn More
    • Automotive Insulation Felt Production Line

      Automotive insulation felt production line primarily utilizes hot air consolidation to manufacture nonwoven materials with a high-density three-dimensional network structure. The product made by airlaid web formation possesses better 3D fiber orientation, enhancing the thermal insulation properties.

      Learn More
    Chemical Bonding Line
    • Spray Bonded Wadding Production Line

      Chemical bonding technology is primarily employed to produce nonwoven materials with a three-dimensional network structure, characterized by fluffy texture.

      Learn More
    • Scouring Pad Production Line

      Scouring pad production line utilizes a combination of chemical bonding and hot air drying processes to produce cleaning cloth products.

      Learn More
    • Chemical Steeping and Bonding Production Line

      Chemical steeping and bonding production line processes synthetic fiber raw materials through opening, blending, carding, web forming, further carding, saturation impregnation, drying and shaping, and winding to produce rolls of fabric.

      Learn More
    Other Nonwoven Production Lines
    • Carpet Backing Line (Stenter Machine)

      The functionalities of the carpet backing line encompass back-coating or impregnation treatment, width stretching and shaping treatment, and composite processing. Customized designs can diversify its functionalities to adapt to different production needs based on the characteristics of different products and technical processing requirements.

      Learn More
    • Textile Waste Recycling Line

      Textile waste recycling line consists of multiple opening machines, and the configuration can be tailored to the composition and condition of waste fabrics. These machines progressively open the fabric to achieve more thorough opening while controlling fiber damage.

    What is the maximum production capacity for nonwoven production line?

    Our nonwoven production line offers a production capacity of 300-1000kg/hr. However, it also depends on the production line’s configuration, and the product’s width, raw material, final product’s weight, etc.

    What are the space and facility requirements for the nonwoven production line?

    The space requirement depends on the production line configuration. For example, a standard 4-metre needle punching line typically requires a dedicated space of at least 20m x 20m, with an open height of 4.5m. Once the configuration is finalized, we will provide a layout drawing to assist with the equipment layout plan. The facility requirements include: power supply, process heating (thermobonding line), compressed air system, cooling water, lighting, etc.

    How long is the delivery time for the production line?

    The standard delivery time for a complete set of the nonwoven production line is 3 to 5 months, depending on the configurations.

    How many containers are needed for the shipment of the nonwoven production line?

    It depends on the configuration, the standard production line generally needs 5 to 8 units of 40-inch high cube containers (40” HQ) for shipping.

    How to install the nonwoven production line’s equipment?

    We will dispatch professional engineers to your facility for installation and commissioning to ensure each machine run successfully. They will also provide professional training about daily operations and maintenance procedures.
